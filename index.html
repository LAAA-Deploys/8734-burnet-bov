02The Value-Creation Strategy

The return is the spread between the rental-basis acquisition (~$205K/unit) and the for-sale value of a renovated condominium (~$495K for a 2BR, ~$625K for a 3BR). How that spread is captured depends on the legal path: the listing represents the subdivision as already in place — a fast HOA reactivation — but if the title report shows otherwise it becomes a full Tentative-Tract-Map conversion. The model lets you compare both.

Step 4 — Sell

Retail Out

Market and sell 20 individual condominiums, repaying the loan and returning equity plus profit as units close.

05Reactivation vs. Full Conversion

Same building, same ~$10.9M sellout — the two legal paths differ only in cost, time, and risk. Reactivating an existing recorded subdivision is far faster and cheaper than a ground-up conversion. Which one applies is settled by the preliminary title report.

07Key Risks & Contingencies

These items drive the spread between the cases — and the first one is the master variable.

1. The legal path — reactivation vs. conversion

The MLS represents the property as built and subdivided as condominiums in 1990 with a dormant HOA, which would make this a fast reactivation. If the preliminary title report instead shows no recorded condominium plan, it reverts to a full conversion — a Tentative Tract Map, a 12-24 month entitlement, and a binary approval gate tied to the area's rental vacancy rate (must be ≥5%, LAMC 12.95.2). That single fact is worth ~$0.8M of profit and ~13 months. Order the title report first.

2. City 9A required work — scope unknown

The counter makes the buyer responsible for the work in the City's Report (9A) after closing. The model carries a $150K placeholder. The actual scope must be obtained and priced in the 12-day contingency window — it could be materially higher.

3. Tenant relocation & occupancy

15 of the 20 units are occupied (5 vacant). Delivering units for individual sale requires tenant turnover with proper notice and relocation assistance. The 1990 build is non-RSO, which eases this, but LA's non-RSO relocation rules still apply — verify amounts and tenant categories with counsel against the rent roll.

4. Absorption against new supply

Valley Villas is delivering 75 new-construction condos in the same ZIP. Selling 20 units into that competition may require price concessions or a longer sell-through — both modeled via the exit-value and timeline inputs.

5. The decision clock

The counter expires 7:00 PM, May 17, 2026; contingencies must be removed 12 calendar days after acceptance, with the 3% deposit ($123,000) at risk after that. The title and 9A diligence must fit inside that window.

08Diligence Checklist — 12-Day Window

What to confirm before removing contingencies, to replace the model's assumptions with deal facts:

  • Order the preliminary title report — confirm whether a condominium plan / subdivision map is recorded (this decides reactivation vs. conversion).
  • Confirm the 8734 Burnet HOA's corporate status (CA Secretary of State / Franchise Tax Board) and the cost to revive it.
  • Obtain the City's Report (9A) and price the required work with a contractor.
  • Get the rent roll — verify current income, occupancy, and lease terms on the 15 occupied units.
  • Engage land-use / condominium counsel for a path opinion (reactivation feasibility, or a TTM timeline if conversion).
  • Confirm tenant relocation exposure (amounts, categories, lease status) against the rent roll.
  • Lock acquisition & renovation loan terms (rate, points, LTC, term).
